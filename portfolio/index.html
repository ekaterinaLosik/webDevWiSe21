The augmented reality application »Oceania«

Climate Change in the Ethnological Museum: Exploring local impacts and agency in the South Pacific with augmented reality. Extending the discourses around historical exhibits of ethnological collections with contemporary worldwide issues such as climate change enrich the museum experience by creating a common plateau between cultures. How augmented reality, through immersive experiences, expands the narrative of the Oceania Collections in the new site of the Ethnologisches Museum in Berlin, the Humboldt Forum. This is done through real-time visualizations of climate change phenomena relying on international research data.

The mixed reality installation TRANSIENT EXPOSURE

TRANSIENT EXPOSURE is a dance and research project in mixed reality. It is a collaboration between the artists’ collective reVerb and the dance company Chitrasena, Colombo (Sri Lanka) – and from January to September 2021 also in collaboration with XR_Unites. Across the distance, it is an attempt to bring together five female artists on different continents. Mixed Reality (MR) becomes a testing ground for a working process with the long-term goal of combining live performance, interaction and mediality. The purely digital installation planned for September 2021 will be a station on the path of realizing TRANSIENT EXPOSURE. A live performance in Colombo and Berlin, planned for 2022, will be another stage.
